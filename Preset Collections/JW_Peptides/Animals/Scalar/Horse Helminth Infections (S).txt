Equine helminth infections, commonly requiring deworming, are caused by ingestion of infective larval stages from contaminated pasture, water, or feed, leading to establishment of gastrointestinal parasites. Primary pathogens include cyathostomins (small strongyles, now the most prevalent and problematic due to widespread resistance), Parascaris species (ascarids, especially in foals), Anoplocephala perfoliata (tapeworms), and occasionally Oxyuris equi (pinworms) or large strongyles (largely controlled historically). Symptoms range from subclinical weight loss, poor coat condition, and reduced performance to severe colic, diarrhea, anemia, and life-threatening larval cyathostominosis (massive synchronous emergence of encysted larvae causing intense intestinal inflammation and protein-losing enteropathy).
