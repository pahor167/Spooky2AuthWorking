Adults can grow up to 12 inches long, with females producing microfilariae that circulate in the blood. The disease progresses in stages: early asymptomatic, mild with cough and fatigue, moderate with exercise intolerance and weight loss, and severe with right heart failure, pulmonary hypertension, anemia, renal dysfunction, ascites, and caval syndrome (worms in the right atrium causing hemolysis and shock).
