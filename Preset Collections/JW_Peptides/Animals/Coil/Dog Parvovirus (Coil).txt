Dog Parvovirus, also known as Canine Parvovirus (CPV), is a highly contagious viral disease primarily affecting dogs, especially puppies and unvaccinated adults. It is caused by the Canine Parvovirus type 2 (CPV-2), a single-stranded DNA virus from the Parvoviridae family, which spreads through direct contact with infected feces or contaminated environments. Symptoms include severe vomiting, bloody diarrhea, lethargy, anorexia, fever, and rapid dehydration, often leading to shock and death if untreated. The pathophysiology involves the virus targeting rapidly dividing cells in the bone marrow, lymphoid tissues, and intestinal crypt epithelium, resulting in leukopenia, immunosuppression, villous atrophy, and secondary bacterial infections that exacerbate gastrointestinal damage.
