This leads to impaired telomerase function or telomere shelterin complex stability, accelerating telomere attrition with each cell division. Clinically, classic DC presents with the diagnostic triad of abnormal skin pigmentation, nail dystrophy, and oral leukoplakia; however, the hallmark complication is bone marrow failure (aplastic anemia) in ~80-90% of cases, often by adolescence or early adulthood. Additional manifestations include pulmonary fibrosis, liver cirrhosis, esophageal stenosis, premature hair graying, increased cancer predisposition (especially squamous cell carcinomas and hematologic malignancies), developmental delay, and immune deficiencies.
