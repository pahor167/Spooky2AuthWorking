The condition is often de novo, with prevalence estimated at 1:50,000-100,000, and diagnosis confirmed by genetic testing showing RPS6KA3 variants.

Pathophysiology revolves around disrupted mitogen-activated signaling cascades leading to deficits in long-term synaptic potentiation, impaired gene expression in neurons and bone cells, and altered cellular responses to extracellular stimuli.
