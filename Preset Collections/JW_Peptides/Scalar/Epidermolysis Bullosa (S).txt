Epidermolysis bullosa (EB) encompasses a group of rare inherited mechanobullous disorders characterized by extreme skin and mucosal fragility leading to blistering, erosions, and wounding from minimal friction or trauma. Mutations in at least 16 genes encoding structural proteins of the cutaneous basement membrane zone disrupt skin integrity across four major types: EB simplex (intraepidermal cleavage, often KRT5/KRT14 mutations affecting keratin intermediate filaments), junctional EB (junctional cleavage, commonly LAMA3/LAMB3/LAMC2 or integrin mutations impairing hemidesmosomes), dystrophic EB (sub-lamina densa cleavage, COL7A1 mutations causing defective type VII collagen anchoring fibrils), and Kindler EB (mixed cleavage levels, FERMT1 mutations disrupting kindlin-1/integrin activation).
