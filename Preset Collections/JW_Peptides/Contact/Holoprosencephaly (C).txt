Holoprosencephaly (HPE) is a severe congenital malformation of the forebrain characterized by incomplete cleavage of the prosencephalon into distinct cerebral hemispheres, occurring between the 18th and 28th days of gestation. It presents on a spectrum from alobar (complete failure of division, single ventricle, absent interhemispheric fissure) to semilobar (partial division) and lobar (near-complete division with fused frontal lobes), with middle interhemispheric variant as a milder form. Causes include genetic mutations in key developmental genes (e.g., SHH, ZIC2, SIX3, TGIF) leading to disrupted signaling pathways like Sonic Hedgehog (SHH), Nodal/TGF{beta}, and FGF, as well as environmental factors such as maternal diabetes, alcohol exposure, or teratogens like cyclopamine.
