These conditions arise from a combination of genetic predispositions, lifestyle factors such as poor diet and physical inactivity, and secondary causes like diabetes, hypothyroidism, or nephrotic syndrome. The pathophysiology involves disrupted cholesterol biosynthesis, impaired lipid absorption and transport, reduced clearance of atherogenic particles, oxidative stress, endothelial dysfunction, and chronic inflammation, leading to the accumulation of lipids in arterial walls. Symptoms may be absent until advanced complications occur, such as coronary artery disease, stroke, peripheral artery disease, or pancreatitis in severe hypertriglyceridemia. Untreated dyslipidemias significantly increase cardiovascular morbidity and mortality, with hypercholesterolemia alone contributing to millions of deaths annually worldwide.
