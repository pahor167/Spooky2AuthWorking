Oxalate Detoxification

Oxalate detoxification addresses conditions of elevated oxalic acid or oxalate levels in the body, leading to hyperoxaluria (excess urinary oxalate) and associated complications. Primary hyperoxaluria is a rare genetic disorder involving defects in glyoxylate metabolism (e.g., AGXT, GRHPR, or HOGA1 mutations), causing hepatic overproduction of oxalate. Secondary hyperoxaluria arises from increased dietary intake (e.g., from spinach, nuts, chocolate), enteric overabsorption (e.g., in inflammatory bowel disease, bariatric surgery, or fat malabsorption), or other factors like ethylene glycol poisoning. Symptoms include recurrent calcium oxalate kidney stones, nephrolithiasis, nephrocalcinosis, chronic kidney disease progression, and in severe cases, end-stage renal failure with systemic oxalosis affecting bones, heart, and tissues.
