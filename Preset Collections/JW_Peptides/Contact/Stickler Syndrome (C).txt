These mutations disrupt normal collagen assembly and extracellular matrix integrity in tissues such as the vitreous humor, cartilage, and craniofacial structures, leading to haploinsufficiency or dominant-negative effects. Symptoms typically include severe myopia, abnormal vitreous gel architecture predisposing to rhegmatogenous retinal detachment (the leading cause of inherited childhood blindness from this complication), sensorineural or mixed hearing loss, early-onset osteoarthritis with joint pain and hypermobility, and orofacial features such as cleft palate or Pierre Robin sequence with midfacial hypoplasia. The pathophysiology centers on defective collagen fibrils impairing mechanical strength and developmental signaling in affected tissues, resulting in progressive multisystem degeneration.
