Kearns-Sayre syndrome (KSS) is a rare multisystem mitochondrial disorder characterized by onset before age 20 with the classic triad of progressive external ophthalmoplegia, pigmentary retinopathy, and cardiac conduction defects, often progressing to complete heart block. Common additional features include cerebellar ataxia, elevated cerebrospinal fluid protein (>100 mg/dL), sensorineural hearing loss, proximal myopathy, endocrine abnormalities (e.g., diabetes mellitus, hypoparathyroidism, growth hormone deficiency), short stature, cognitive impairment, and renal tubular dysfunction.
