Méničre’s disease is a chronic idiopathic inner ear disorder characterized by recurrent episodic vertigo lasting minutes to hours, fluctuating sensorineural hearing loss typically low-frequency, persistent or intermittent tinnitus, and a sensation of aural fullness or pressure in the affected ear, usually unilateral initially but potentially bilateral over time. Causes remain incompletely understood but involve multifactorial contributions including genetic predispositions (familial in ~10% of cases with autosomal patterns), environmental triggers, allergic or autoinflammatory responses, vascular dysregulation, and disruptions in inner ear fluid homeostasis; secondary associations include migraine overlap and prior head trauma. Symptoms profoundly impair quality of life with unpredictable attacks causing nausea, imbalance, and progressive hearing impairment potentially leading to severe deafness, alongside psychological burden from chronic uncertainty.
