Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (EGPA), formerly known as Churg-Strauss Syndrome, is a rare systemic autoimmune vasculitis classified among the ANCA-associated vasculitides. It typically presents in three phases: a prodromal phase with adult-onset asthma, allergic rhinitis, and sinusitis; an eosinophilic phase with marked blood and tissue eosinophilia affecting lungs, gastrointestinal tract, and skin; and a vasculitic phase with life-threatening small-to-medium vessel necrotizing vasculitis involving peripheral nerves, heart, kidneys, and other organs. Symptoms include severe asthma exacerbations, nasal polyps, pulmonary infiltrates, mononeuritis multiplex, skin purpura or nodules, cardiac dysfunction (leading cause of mortality), abdominal pain, and fatigue. Untreated or severe cases carry high morbidity due to organ damage from eosinophil degranulation (releasing toxic proteins like major basic protein and eosinophil cationic protein) and ischemic complications from vasculitis.

Complement activation (C5a) amplifies inflammation in subsets, while B-cell dysregulation sustains autoantibody production. Cardiac involvement reflects direct eosinophilic infiltration and granuloma formation.
