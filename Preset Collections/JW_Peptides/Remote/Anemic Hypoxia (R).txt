Anemic hypoxia is a subtype of hypoxia defined by diminished oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood, resulting from low hemoglobin concentration or dysfunctional hemoglobin unable to bind/release oxygen effectively, despite normal alveolar ventilation and arterial PO2. Primary causes encompass iron-deficiency anemia, megaloblastic anemias (vitamin B12/folate deficiency), anemia of chronic disease or inflammation, renal insufficiency with deficient erythropoietin production, hemolytic disorders, hemoglobinopathies (e.g., sickle cell disease, thalassemias), and toxic/metabolic impairments (carbon monoxide forming carboxyhemoglobin, drug-induced methemoglobinemia). Clinical manifestations include progressive fatigue, pallor, exertional dyspnea, tachycardia, dizziness, cognitive fog, and in severe untreated cases, angina, heart failure, or multi-organ ischemia.
