The disease presents in three major clinical forms: cutaneous leishmaniasis (CL), the most common, characterized by skin lesions, ulcers, and scars; mucocutaneous leishmaniasis (MCL), involving destructive lesions in mucous membranes of the nose, mouth, and throat; and visceral leishmaniasis (VL, also known as kala-azar), the deadliest, targeting internal organs like the spleen, liver, and bone marrow, leading to irregular fever, weight loss, hepatosplenomegaly, anemia, pancytopenia, and immunosuppression, with fatality rates up to 95% if untreated.
