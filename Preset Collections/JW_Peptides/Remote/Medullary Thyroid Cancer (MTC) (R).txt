Medullary thyroid cancer (MTC) is a rare neuroendocrine malignancy arising from the parafollicular C-cells of the thyroid gland, accounting for approximately 1-4% of all thyroid cancers. It occurs in sporadic (70-80%) and hereditary (20-30%) forms, the latter associated with multiple endocrine neoplasia type 2 (MEN2A/MEN2B) syndromes or familial MTC due to germline RET proto-oncogene mutations. Sporadic cases often involve somatic RET mutations, with additional alterations in RAS, HRAS/KRAS, or rarely ALK fusions. Causes center on activating mutations driving constitutive oncogenic signaling, leading to uncontrolled proliferation, survival, and metastasis.
