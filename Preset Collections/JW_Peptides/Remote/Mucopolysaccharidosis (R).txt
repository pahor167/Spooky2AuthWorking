Mucopolysaccharidosis (MPS) refers to a group of rare inherited lysosomal storage disorders caused by deficiencies in specific enzymes responsible for breaking down glycosaminoglycans (GAGs), such as heparan sulfate, dermatan sulfate, keratan sulfate, and chondroitin sulfate. Genetic mutations in genes encoding these lysosomal enzymes lead to progressive GAG accumulation in various tissues and organs, resulting in multisystemic damage. Symptoms typically manifest in early childhood and include coarse facial features, skeletal deformities (dysostosis multiplex), joint stiffness, hepatosplenomegaly, cardiac valve abnormalities, respiratory complications, corneal clouding, hearing loss, and in many types, progressive neurological decline with cognitive impairment, behavioral issues, and hydrocephalus.
