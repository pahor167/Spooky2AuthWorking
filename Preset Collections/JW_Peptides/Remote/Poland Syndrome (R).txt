Poland Syndrome (also known as Poland anomaly or pectoral hand syndrome) is a rare congenital disorder occurring in approximately 1 in 20,000 to 100,000 births, characterized primarily by unilateral hypoplasia or aplasia of the pectoralis major muscle, often with associated ipsilateral upper extremity anomalies. Common features include absence of the sternocostal head of the pectoralis major, hypoplasia of the pectoralis minor, subcutaneous tissue deficiency, breast/nipple-areola complex underdevelopment (particularly in females), and hand deformities such as syndactyly, brachydactyly, ectrodactyly, or hypoplasia of the middle phalanges. Less frequently, it involves rib cage anomalies, dextrocardia, or lung herniation. The condition is typically sporadic, more common on the right side and in males, with rare familial clustering.
