Mycotoxicosis is the clinical syndrome resulting from exposure to mycotoxins, toxic secondary metabolites produced by molds such as Aspergillus, Fusarium, and Penicillium species contaminating crops, foods, and feeds. Major mycotoxins include aflatoxin B1 (hepatocarcinogenic), ochratoxin A (nephrotoxic), zearalenone (estrogen-like reproductive effects), fumonisins (neurotoxic), and trichothecenes such as deoxynivalenol (DON, causes emesis and feed refusal) and T-2 toxin (severe cytotoxicity). Acute exposure leads to symptoms like vomiting, diarrhea, anorexia, hemorrhage, and immune suppression; chronic exposure contributes to organ damage (liver, kidney), growth impairment, immunosuppression, and increased cancer risk. Central pathophysiology involves generation of reactive oxygen species leading to oxidative stress, lipid peroxidation, mitochondrial dysfunction, apoptosis, inflammation, protein synthesis inhibition, and disruption of cellular redox homeostasis, with Nrf2 pathway impairment exacerbating damage.

The proposed polypeptides target pivotal mechanisms of mycotoxin toxicity for therapeutic intervention. Real Gly-Pro-Ala peptide, identified from fish skin gelatin hydrolysate, alleviates DON-induced oxidative stress and intestinal injury by promoting Nrf2 nuclear translocation and upregulation of antioxidant enzymes in preclinical mouse and cellular models. Real SWGPFPF mimotope, selected via phage display against DON-specific antibody, exhibits high-affinity binding to DON and antagonizes its inhibition of protein synthesis in vitro. Designed capped Nrf2-Keap1 disruptor, rationally derived from the Nrf2 Neh2 domain's high-affinity Keap1-binding motif (LDEETGEFL core) with terminal N-acetylation and C-amidation for enhanced proteolytic stability, aims to disrupt Keap1 inhibition and amplify endogenous Nrf2-mediated antioxidant defenses against mycotoxin-induced ROS. Designed lysine-flanked DON binder, based on SWGPFPF motif with flanking lysines and terminal capping for improved solubility and mucoadhesive properties, facilitates gastrointestinal sequestration to reduce mycotoxin absorption and bioavailability.
