Parkinson-plus syndromes, also known as atypical parkinsonian disorders, encompass a group of neurodegenerative conditions including multiple system atrophy (MSA), progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP), and corticobasal degeneration (CBD). These disorders present with parkinsonism (bradykinesia, rigidity, postural instability) plus additional features such as supranuclear gaze palsy and early falls in PSP, autonomic failure and cerebellar signs in MSA, and asymmetric cortical signs in CBD, with generally poor response to levodopa therapy. Pathophysiology involves abnormal protein aggregation ({alpha}-synuclein glial inclusions in MSA, tau neurofibrillary tangles in PSP/CBD), neuroinflammation with microglial activation, mitochondrial dysfunction, oxidative stress, impaired protein clearance, and selective neuronal loss in basal ganglia, brainstem, and cortical regions, leading to rapid progression and high disability.

The proposed polypeptides comprehensively target core mechanisms across these heterogeneous syndromes. Davunetide, a real-world octapeptide investigated in clinical trials for PSP, stabilizes microtubules and exerts neuroprotective effects against tau-related toxicity. The designed cyclic tau-inhibitor, derived from the aggregation-prone PHF6 motif (VQIVYK) with proline substitutions to disrupt {beta}-sheet formation and head-to-tail cyclization for enhanced stability, interferes with tau fibrillization. The designed {alpha}-syn helical stabilizer, based on N-terminal {alpha}-synuclein sequences that promote physiological helical structure with head-to-tail cyclization for improved pharmacokinetics, prevents pathogenic misfolding and aggregation in synucleinopathies. VIP (vasoactive intestinal peptide), a real-world neuropeptide with preclinical evidence in parkinsonian models, suppresses neuroinflammation and supports neuronal survival through receptor-mediated immunomodulation. The designed cationic modulator, derived from innate defense regulator peptide motifs with C-amide for stability, dampens excessive inflammatory signaling in the CNS. Elamipretide, a real-world mitochondria-targeted tetrapeptide with evidence in neurodegenerative models, binds cardiolipin to stabilize electron transport and reduce reactive oxygen species. The designed aromatic-cationic polypeptide, inspired by mitochondria-penetrating motifs with alternating aromatic and cationic residues plus C-amide for drug-likeness, scavenges mitochondrial ROS and supports bioenergetic function.
