Stage 4 colon cancer, also known as metastatic colorectal cancer, is the most advanced stage of the disease where the cancer has spread from the colon to distant sites such as the liver, lungs, peritoneum, bones, or other organs. The primary causes include genetic mutations in genes like APC, KRAS, BRAF, TP53, and PI3K, which lead to uncontrolled cell growth and spread. Environmental factors such as a high-fat diet, smoking, alcohol consumption, lack of physical activity, and chronic inflammatory conditions like ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease increase risk. Symptoms often include persistent abdominal pain, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, blood in the stool, changes in bowel habits (diarrhea or constipation), nausea, vomiting, and jaundice or swelling if the liver is involved. Pathophysiology begins with the adenoma-carcinoma sequence, where benign polyps become malignant due to accumulated genetic alterations. This leads to invasion of the bowel wall, lymph node involvement, and distant metastasis through processes like epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT), angiogenesis via VEGF signaling, immune evasion, chemokine-mediated migration (e.g., CXCL12-CXCR4), and activation of survival pathways like PI3K/Akt/mTOR. The cancer cells gain the ability to survive in new environments, form secondary tumors, and resist therapy, resulting in poor prognosis without treatment.

The proposed polypeptides address stage 4 colon cancer through multiple mechanisms, targeting key pathophysiological pathways to inhibit tumor growth, metastasis, angiogenesis, and immune evasion, while promoting apoptosis and immune recognition. The vaccine peptides (e.g., VEGFR1 and VEGFR2 epitopes) elicit cytotoxic T-lymphocyte responses against angiogenesis-promoting cells, reducing blood supply to tumors. KRAS mutant peptides stimulate immune attacks on oncogenic signaling, disrupting cell proliferation. Oncolytic peptides like LTX-315 and mellitin directly lyse cancer cells, releasing antigens to boost immunity. Lunasin induces apoptosis and inhibits metastasis by interfering with chromatin and cell adhesion. MUC1-based peptides enhance immune recognition of tumor-associated antigens. Other epitope peptides target cell cycle, stemness, chromosome stability, tumor growth, and metabolism to collectively slow disease progression. The designed polypeptides supplement coverage: D1 targets PI3K/Akt/mTOR to block survival signaling; D2 inhibits CXCL12-CXCR4 to prevent metastasis; D3 disrupts HER2 signaling for growth inhibition; D4 modulates Activin A/ACVR2A to suppress EMT.
