Burns are injuries to the skin and underlying tissues primarily caused by thermal sources (e.g., flame, scald, contact), but also chemical, electrical, or radiation exposure, leading to coagulative necrosis and varying depths of damage classified as superficial, partial-thickness, or full-thickness. Pathophysiology involves immediate cellular destruction from protein denaturation, vascular thrombosis in the zone of stasis (potentially convertible to necrosis if untreated), massive inflammatory cytokine release triggering local edema and systemic responses in severe cases (>20% TBSA), hypermetabolic state with catabolism, immunosuppression increasing infection risk due to barrier loss, oxidative stress, and delayed re-epithelialization with potential hypertrophic scarring or contractures. Symptoms range from pain, erythema, and blistering in superficial burns to painless charring and eschar in full-thickness, with severe burns causing hypovolemic shock, sepsis, or multi-organ failure.
