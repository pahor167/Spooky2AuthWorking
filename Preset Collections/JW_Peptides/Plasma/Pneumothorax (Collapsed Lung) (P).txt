Pneumothorax is the presence of air in the pleural space leading to partial or complete lung collapse, classified as primary spontaneous (PSP, often in young tall males due to subpleural bleb/bullae rupture without underlying lung disease), secondary (SSP, complicating conditions like COPD, cystic fibrosis, LAM, or Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome), traumatic, or iatrogenic. Symptoms range from mild chest pain and dyspnea to life-threatening tension physiology with hypotension, tracheal deviation, and cardiovascular compromise. Pathophysiology involves loss of negative intrapleural pressure, impaired ventilation-perfusion matching, hypoxemia, and in recurrent cases, persistent air leaks from visceral pleural defects; pro-fibrotic and inflammatory responses play roles in natural resolution or complications, with pathways like TGF-{beta} driving pleural adhesion in experimental pleurodesis.

Peptide 2 (designed) is arginine-phenylalanine rich amphipathic for potential cell penetration and repair signaling at air leak sites. Peptide 3 (designed) is glutamine-serine rich polar to conceptually dampen pleural inflammatory cascades. Peptide 4 (designed) is tyrosine-tryptophan rich aromatic to conceptually stabilize parenchymal tissue and resist bleb formation. Peptide 5 (designed) is lysine-histidine rich cationic to conceptually support balanced immunomodulation during healing. These diverse short designs prioritize synthetic feasibility, stability, and potential intrapleural or aerosol delivery as adjuncts to standard mechanical interventions.
