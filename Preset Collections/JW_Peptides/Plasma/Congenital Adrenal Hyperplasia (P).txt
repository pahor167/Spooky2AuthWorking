Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH) is a group of autosomal recessive genetic disorders primarily caused by mutations in genes encoding enzymes involved in cortisol biosynthesis, with the most common form being 21-hydroxylase deficiency due to CYP21A2 mutations, affecting approximately 1 in 10,000 to 15,000 live births worldwide. This deficiency disrupts the conversion of 17-hydroxyprogesterone to 11-deoxycortisol, leading to impaired cortisol and often aldosterone production, while shunting precursors toward excess androgen synthesis. Symptoms manifest as salt-wasting crises in severe cases (with hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, dehydration, and shock in neonates), ambiguous genitalia in females, rapid growth with early epiphyseal closure leading to short stature, precocious puberty, hirsutism, acne, infertility, and metabolic issues like insulin resistance in adults. Pathophysiology revolves around cortisol deficiency triggering elevated ACTH via loss of negative feedback on the HPA axis, resulting in adrenal hyperplasia, androgen excess, and mineralocorticoid deficiency in classic forms, while non-classic CAH presents milder, later-onset hyperandrogenism without salt-wasting.

The proposed polypeptides address CAH through therapeutic axes including HPA axis modulation to suppress excess ACTH, gonadal androgen suppression to manage hyperandrogenism and pubertal timing, direct steroidogenesis modulation to reduce androgen precursors, growth optimization to counteract short stature, and mineralocorticoid pathway support to stabilize electrolyte balance. Real-world polypeptides like cosyntropin aid in HPA stimulation for diagnostic and acute management, while leuprolide suppresses pituitary-gonadal axis to delay puberty and reduce androgen effects. Designed polypeptides D1, D2, and D3 are conceptualized to bind CRF receptors reducing ACTH, inhibit key steroidogenic enzymes like CYP17, and enhance growth factor signaling, respectively, potentially normalizing hormone levels, improving height outcomes, and minimizing glucocorticoid overtreatment side effects.
