Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a progressive, preventable respiratory disorder characterized by persistent airflow limitation and respiratory symptoms due to airway and/or alveolar abnormalities, primarily caused by long-term exposure to noxious particles or gases, most commonly cigarette smoke, biomass fuels, occupational dusts, or air pollution. Genetic factors (e.g., alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency) and early-life events (poor lung growth) contribute in subsets. Symptoms include chronic cough, sputum production, dyspnea on exertion progressing to rest, wheeze, chest tightness, fatigue, weight loss, and frequent exacerbations (worsening triggered by infections or pollutants) leading to hospitalizations and accelerated decline. Pathophysiology involves multifaceted chronic inflammation (predominantly neutrophilic/macrophage-driven with CD8+ T cells, variable eosinophilic in subsets), oxidative stress from reactive species overwhelming antioxidants, protease-antiprotease imbalance (elevated MMPs, neutrophil elastase degrading elastin), impaired mucociliary clearance with goblet cell hyperplasia/mucus hypersecretion, small airway fibrosis/obstruction, alveolar wall destruction (emphysema) with loss of elastic recoil, vascular remodeling, systemic comorbidities (cardiovascular, skeletal muscle wasting), and dysregulated repair with mitochondrial dysfunction/cell death pathways perpetuating cycles despite irritant removal.

Current treatments (bronchodilators, inhaled corticosteroids, PDE4 inhibitors, mucolytics) provide symptomatic relief and reduce exacerbations but do not reverse damage or halt progression; emerging biologics target eosinophilic subsets but not core neutrophilic mechanisms or remodeling. The proposed designed polypeptides introduce conceptual topical/inhaled alternatives addressing persistent inflammation, oxidative burden, and remodeling with enhanced stability for pulmonary delivery. All are designed: the VIP analog derives from vasoactive intestinal peptide motif (depleted in COPD lungs, preclinical evidence for bronchodilation, cytokine suppression, and macrophage polarization via VPAC receptors) with C-amidation; the alpha-MSH analog builds on melanocortin peptide scaffold (known suppression of NF-{kappa}B-driven inflammation and oxidative stress in lung models) with N-acetylation and C-amidation for protease resistance; the catestatin analog stems from chromogranin A-derived motif (inhibits catecholamine release, stabilizes mast cells, antimicrobial against exacerbating pathogens like bacteria in literature) with C-amidation.
