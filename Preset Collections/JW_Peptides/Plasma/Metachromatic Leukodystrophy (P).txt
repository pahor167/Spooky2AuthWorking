Metachromatic leukodystrophy (MLD) is a rare autosomal-recessive lysosomal storage disorder caused by mutations in the ARSA gene leading to deficient arylsulfatase A enzyme activity. Accumulation of sulfatide lipids in central and peripheral nervous system myelin triggers progressive demyelination, neuroinflammation, and neurodegeneration. Clinical variants include late-infantile, juvenile, and adult forms, all culminating in motor and cognitive decline. 
The proposed polypeptide set targets six mechanistic axes: (1) ARSA-mimetic peptide designed to restore catalytic activity within lysosomes; (2) saposin-B-mimetic designed to enhance residual ARSA folding and stability; (3) lysosomal-export enhancer designed to accelerate sulfatide efflux; (4) neurotrophic mimic designed to support oligodendrocyte survival and axonal integrity; (5) microglial-quieting motif designed to attenuate neuroinflammation; (6) OPC-differentiation peptide designed to promote remyelination. All six peptides are designed de novo to fit strict length and diversity rules while addressing the multifactorial pathology of MLD.
