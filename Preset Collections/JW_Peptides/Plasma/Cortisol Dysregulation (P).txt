Cortisol dysregulation refers to aberrant levels, rhythms, or responsiveness to cortisol, the main glucocorticoid regulated by the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. It includes hypercortisolism (e.g., Cushing's syndrome from pituitary/adrenal tumors, ectopic ACTH, or chronic stress) and hypocortisolism (e.g., primary/secondary adrenal insufficiency). Hypocortisolism leads to fatigue, weight loss, hypotension, electrolyte imbalances, and adrenal crisis. Core pathophysiology involves impaired CRH-ACTH-cortisol negative feedback, receptor desensitization, and downstream inflammatory/metabolic effects.
