Minor head injury (mild traumatic brain injury or concussion) involves acute biomechanical forces leading to transient brain dysfunction, with risks of secondary injury including neuroinflammation, oxidative stress, apoptosis, and cognitive deficits. Proposed polypeptides target key therapeutic axes derived from preclinical models:

1. Targeted binding to upregulated extracellular matrix in injured brain
2. Anti-apoptotic neuroprotection via erythropoietin mimicry
3. Neuroinflammation suppression via apolipoprotein E mimicry
4. Oxidative stress reduction via thioredoxin mimicry
5. Neurotrophic support and cognitive recovery via GLP-1 receptor agonism

Assignments and mechanisms:
- CAQK (real, tetrapeptide identified by phage display): Binds specifically to tenascin C in injured brain tissue, reduces lesion volume, apoptosis, neuroinflammation, and improves functional outcomes in TBI models.
- pHBSP (real, erythropoietin-derived): Mimics non-hematopoietic EPO effects, reduces inflammatory activation, and enhances cognitive recovery in mild TBI rat models.
- CN-105 (real, ApoE-mimetic pentapeptide): Suppresses sterile immune activation, reduces histological damage, and improves functional outcomes in closed head injury models.
- TXM-CB3 (real, thioredoxin-mimetic): Redox-active peptide that protects cognitive function and reduces neuronal damage post-mTBI via antioxidant effects.
- Exendin-4 (real, natural GLP-1 receptor agonist peptide): Activates CREB pathway, reduces oxidative stress/excitotoxicity, mitigates cognitive and sensory deficits in mild TBI/concussion models.
