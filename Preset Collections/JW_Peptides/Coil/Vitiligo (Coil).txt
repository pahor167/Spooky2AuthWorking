Vitiligo (Autoimmune Depigmentation Disorder)

Vitiligo is a chronic autoimmune disorder characterized by selective destruction of melanocytes, resulting in patchy depigmentation of skin and hair. Pathogenesis involves autoreactive CD8+ T cells targeting melanocyte antigens (tyrosinase, gp100), oxidative stress-induced melanocyte damage, and defective immune tolerance. Current therapeutic strategies focus on immunosuppression (JAK inhibitors, corticosteroids), phototherapy to stimulate residual melanocyte activity, and emerging biologics targeting IL-15 and CXCL10 pathways. Repigmentation requires both cessation of autoimmune attack and activation of melanocyte stem cells from follicular reservoirs.

Therapeutic peptide interventions:
1. Melanocortin stimulation/MC1R activation (Melanogenesis): Afamelanotide - Synthetic {alpha}-MSH analog binding MC1R to stimulate melanocyte proliferation and melanin synthesis; enhanced stability via Nle4 and D-Phe7 substitutions; Phase 3 trials for vitiligo repigmentation
2. Anti-inflammatory/Immunomodulation: KPV - C-terminal fragment of {alpha}-MSH retaining anti-inflammatory activity; suppresses IL-1{beta} and NF{kappa}B signaling; reduces autoimmune melanocyte attack without hyperpigmentation effects
3. Melanocyte migration/proliferation (bFGF pathway): Melgain (IS-103) - Basic fibroblast growth factor fragment stimulating melanocyte migration and proliferation; enhances melanin synthesis via tyrosinase activation; marketed topical for vitiligo repigmentation
4. Oxidative stress reduction: OxiDefend-1 - Cysteine-rich metal-chelating peptide designed to neutralize reactive oxygen species; mimics metallothionein function to protect melanocytes from oxidative damage in vitiligo lesions
5. Treg enhancement/Immune tolerance: TregMod-1 - Designed Tregitope-like sequence to induce regulatory T cell activity; restores immune tolerance and suppresses autoreactive CD8+ T cells attacking melanocytes without broad immunosuppression
6. CXCL10/CXCR3 antagonism (T cell recruitment blockade): CXCR3Ant-1 - CXCR3 receptor antagonist peptide blocking IFN{gamma}-inducible chemokine signaling; prevents pathogenic T cell recruitment to skin lesions; reduces autoimmune inflammation in depigmented patches
7. Wnt/{beta}-catenin pathway activation (Melanocyte stem cell differentiation): WntBoost-1 - Wnt pathway agonist designed to activate {beta}-catenin signaling; promotes differentiation of melanocyte stem cells from follicular reservoirs into functional melanocytes for repigmentation
8. Tyrosinase enhancement/Melanogenic gene expression: MelaninPro-1 - Nuclear-localizing basic peptide designed to upregulate MITF and tyrosinase expression; enhances melanogenic enzyme activity and melanin production in residual melanocytes
9. Nrf2/ARE pathway activation (Antioxidant response): Nrf2Act-1 - Synthetic ETGE-repeat peptide mimicking Keap1 binding motif; displaces Nrf2 from Keap1 to activate antioxidant response element; induces phase II detoxification enzymes in melanocytes
10. Mitochondrial protection/Energy metabolism: MitoShield-1 - Alternating Phe-Lys amphipathic peptide targeting mitochondrial membrane; protects against oxidative phosphorylation dysfunction and ROS generation in stressed melanocytes
