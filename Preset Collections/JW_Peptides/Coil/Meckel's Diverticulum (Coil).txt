It affects approximately 2% of the population, with a male predominance, and often remains asymptomatic, but complications occur in 4-25% of cases, manifesting primarily as gastrointestinal bleeding from peptic ulceration due to ectopic gastric mucosa, intestinal obstruction from intussusception, volvulus, or adhesions, diverticulitis resembling appendicitis with inflammation and potential perforation leading to peritonitis, fistulas, or rarely neoplasia.
