Stunted growth, or short stature, is a condition where an individual's height is significantly below the average for their age, sex, and population, typically defined as below the 3rd percentile or more than 2 standard deviations below the mean on growth charts. Causes include genetic factors such as familial short stature, skeletal dysplasias like achondroplasia due to FGFR3 mutations, or syndromes like Turner, Noonan, or SHORT syndrome involving PIK3R1; endocrine disruptions like growth hormone deficiency (GHD), insulin-like growth factor-1 (IGF-1) deficiency, hypothyroidism, or delayed puberty; nutritional deficiencies from malnutrition, malabsorption, or gut microbiome imbalances affecting butyrate production and gene expression; chronic diseases including chronic kidney disease or intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR); and environmental factors like psychosocial stress or toxin exposure. Symptoms manifest as slowed growth velocity, delayed bone age, disproportionate body proportions in some cases (e.g., short limbs in achondroplasia), delayed puberty, fatigue, and potential psychological impacts from height-related social challenges. Pathophysiologically, it involves impairments in the GH/IGF-1 axis leading to reduced chondrocyte proliferation in growth plates, disrupted pubertal hormone signaling (e.g., kisspeptin-GnRH pathway) delaying epiphyseal fusion timing, altered skeletal signaling (e.g., overactive FGFR3 suppressing endochondral ossification), microbiome dysbiosis reducing short-chain fatty acid-mediated IGF-1 expression, and epigenetic drifts affecting gene rhythms for growth factors.

The proposed polypeptides target these axes to promote linear growth. Hexarelin stimulates the GH axis by acting as a secretagogue to enhance pituitary GH release, improving growth velocity in GHD or ISS. The designed polypeptide for GH axis mimics releasing motifs to boost endogenous GH and IGF-1 signaling. For IGF-1 axis, the designed polypeptide activates downstream pathways to amplify anabolic effects on chondrocytes, bypassing GH resistance. Kisspeptin-10 addresses pubertal delay by stimulating GnRH release, initiating puberty and extending the growth window. The designed polypeptide for pubertal enhances gonadotropic signaling for hormonal balance. Vosoritide targets skeletal development in achondroplasia by antagonizing FGFR3 via CNP pathway activation, promoting chondrocyte hypertrophy and bone elongation. The designed polypeptide for skeletal incorporates motifs to inhibit excessive FGFR3 and support ossification. For microbiome/nutritional axis, the designed polypeptide modulates gut flora to increase butyrate and IGF-1 expression, aiding nutrient-driven growth.
