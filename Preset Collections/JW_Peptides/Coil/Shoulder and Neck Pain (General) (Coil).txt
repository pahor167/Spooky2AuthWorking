Shoulder and neck discomfort is a widespread musculoskeletal issue manifesting as persistent aching, stiffness, tightness, and restricted mobility in the cervical spine, upper trapezius, and surrounding shoulder girdle muscles. Pathophysiologically, it involves sustained low-level muscle contraction causing localized ischemia, development of myofascial trigger points with sensitized nociceptors, release of proinflammatory mediators, impaired proprioceptive feedback, heightened sympathetic tone, and in prolonged cases, central sensitization leading to amplified and widespread pain perception.

The proposed polypeptides comprehensively target the major therapeutic axes of the condition:

1. Stress-induced muscle tension: Psychosocial stress elevates catecholamine levels and sympathetic activity, promoting involuntary muscle guarding and hypertonicity in neck and shoulder regions. GHK, a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide motif, counters this through downregulation of inflammatory signaling, enhancement of antioxidant defenses, and promotion of orderly collagen synthesis as evidenced in tissue repair studies.

4. Central pain sensitization and neuroinflammation: Chronic peripheral input induces spinal and brain changes, lowering pain thresholds. These polypeptides provide multi-axis coverage via regenerative, neuroprotective, and modulatory mechanisms grounded in literature-derived motifs.
