Salicylate intolerance, also known as salicylate sensitivity, is a non-IgE-mediated adverse reaction to naturally occurring salicylates in foods (e.g., fruits, vegetables, spices, herbs) and synthetic forms such as acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin). It affects a significant subset of individuals with asthma, chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps, and related conditions, often manifesting as aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease (AERD, or Samter's triad). Symptoms range from respiratory (bronchospasm, nasal congestion, rhinorrhea, polyps), dermatological (urticaria, angioedema), gastrointestinal (nausea, pain), to systemic pseudo-anaphylactoid reactions.
