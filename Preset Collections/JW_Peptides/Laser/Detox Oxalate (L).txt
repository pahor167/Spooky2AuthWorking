Oxalate detoxification addresses conditions of elevated oxalic acid or oxalate levels in the body, leading to hyperoxaluria (excess urinary oxalate) and associated complications. Primary hyperoxaluria is a rare genetic disorder involving defects in glyoxylate metabolism (e.g., AGXT, GRHPR, or HOGA1 mutations), causing hepatic overproduction of oxalate.
