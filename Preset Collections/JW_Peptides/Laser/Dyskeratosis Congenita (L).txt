Clinically, classic DC presents with the diagnostic triad of abnormal skin pigmentation, nail dystrophy, and oral leukoplakia; however, the hallmark complication is bone marrow failure (aplastic anemia) in ~80-90% of cases, often by adolescence or early adulthood.
