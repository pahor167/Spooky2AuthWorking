The infection is acquired through the consumption of raw or undercooked freshwater fish containing viable plerocercoid larvae. Once ingested, the larvae attach to the mucosa of the small intestine, where they mature into adult tapeworms that can grow up to 10-15 meters in length over several weeks. Risk factors include consumption of raw fish dishes like sushi, sashimi, or ceviche in endemic areas such as Northern Europe, Japan, North America, and parts of Asia and South America.
