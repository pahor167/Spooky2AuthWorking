Hypoxemic hypoxia, also known as arterial hypoxemia leading to tissue hypoxia, is a condition characterized by reduced oxygen content in arterial blood (low PaO2), resulting in inadequate oxygen delivery to tissues despite normal or compensated cardiac output. It manifests with symptoms such as dyspnea, tachycardia, cyanosis, confusion, headache, and in severe cases, organ dysfunction or failure. Common causes include hypoventilation (e.g., opioid overdose, neuromuscular disease), ventilation-perfusion mismatch (e.g., pneumonia, pulmonary embolism), right-to-left shunt (e.g., ARDS, congenital heart defects), diffusion impairment (e.g., interstitial lung disease, pulmonary edema), and low inspired oxygen (e.g., high altitude).
