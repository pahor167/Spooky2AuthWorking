Kearns-Sayre syndrome (KSS) is a rare multisystem mitochondrial disorder characterized by onset before age 20 with the classic triad of progressive external ophthalmoplegia, pigmentary retinopathy, and cardiac conduction defects, often progressing to complete heart block. Common additional features include cerebellar ataxia, elevated cerebrospinal fluid protein (>100 mg/dL), sensorineural hearing loss, proximal myopathy, endocrine abnormalities (e.g., diabetes mellitus, hypoparathyroidism, growth hormone deficiency), short stature, cognitive impairment, and renal tubular dysfunction. The pathophysiology stems primarily from sporadic large-scale single deletions in mitochondrial DNA (most commonly ~4.9 kb), resulting in loss of tRNA and electron transport chain subunit genes, impaired oxidative phosphorylation, defective ATP production, disrupted mitochondrial protein synthesis, increased reactive oxygen species generation, and tissue-specific accumulation of dysfunctional mitochondria in post-mitotic cells with high energy demands, such as retina, extraocular muscles, cardiac conduction system, skeletal muscle, and brain.

The proposed polypeptides target the core mitochondrial dysfunction driving KSS progression. The real-world polypeptide elamipretide (SS-31) is a clinically investigated mitochondria-targeted tetrapeptide that binds cardiolipin, prevents its peroxidation, maintains cristae structure, optimizes electron transport chain activity, and suppresses ROS production, addressing energy failure and oxidative damage in primary mitochondrial diseases including deletion syndromes. The designed polypeptides are conceptual small amphipathic cationic peptides with C-terminal amidation for enhanced stability and mitochondrial accumulation via electrophoretic uptake: the first designed polypeptide features alternating aromatic-cationic residues to support inner membrane integrity; the second incorporates tryptophan for improved membrane interaction and bioenergetics support; the third includes histidine and tyrosine residues for auxiliary ROS neutralization alongside targeting. These designs leverage established physicochemical principles of mitochondria-penetrating peptides, offering feasible complementarity to symptomatic management by ameliorating molecular defects in oxidative phosphorylation, membrane stability, and oxidative stress.
