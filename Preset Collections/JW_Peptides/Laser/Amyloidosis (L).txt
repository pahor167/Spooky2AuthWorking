The condition can be classified into several types based on the precursor protein involved: primary (AL) amyloidosis, stemming from abnormal immunoglobulin light chains produced by clonal plasma cells; secondary (AA) amyloidosis, associated with chronic inflammation where serum amyloid A protein accumulates; hereditary or wild-type transthyretin (ATTR) amyloidosis, involving misfolded transthyretin protein; and other rarer forms like localized amyloidosis. Pathophysiology centers on protein misfolding, where normally soluble proteins adopt beta-sheet-rich conformations, aggregate into protofibrils and fibrils, and deposit extracellularly, triggering inflammation, oxidative stress, cellular toxicity, and mechanical disruption of tissue architecture.
